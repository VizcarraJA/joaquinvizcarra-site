A well-validated wearable. 35 centers. 156 patients. 26 weeks.

Rodríguez-Molinero and colleagues randomized neurologists across 35 Spanish neurology centers into three approaches for guiding PD medication adjustments: clinical visit alone, Hauser diary, or the STAT-ON Parkinson's Holter — a waist-worn device that continuously records motor activity and generates a report showing percentage of time in ON, OFF, and intermediate states, along with freezing episodes and dyskinesia counts.

All three arms moved similarly. OFF time, ON time, dyskinesia, PDQ-8 — comparable across groups.

At first glance, it might seem like continuous motor monitoring adds little. A closer look suggests the findings say more about where the technology was tested than about the technology itself.

Two things stand out.

First, a ceiling effect. These were academic specialist centers. Neurologists who manage high volumes of PD patients and already interpret motor fluctuations with substantial experience. A tool that structures information clinicians already handle well has limited room to shift population-level outcomes in that setting.

Second, the Hawthorne effect. All three arms improved similarly — including the clinical visit arm. Patients in every group were being formally observed, completing more structured monitoring than they would in routine care. When observation elevates all arms simultaneously, the differences between them narrow.

Neither of these explanations dismisses the technology. They locate it more precisely.

The settings where these tools likely have more room to work look different: a general neurologist managing a handful of PD patients across a busy mixed practice, a rural clinic with twice-yearly specialist access, a telehealth model where direct observation is constrained. In those environments, four weeks of structured motor state data provides information the clinical encounter alone may not capture. The information gap is wider, and a tool that closes it has more room to matter.

That is a different research question than the one this trial answered. It may also be the more important one.
